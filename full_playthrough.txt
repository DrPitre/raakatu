RAAKA-TU: A Full Playthrough (Score 50/50)
===========================================

The following is a step-by-step account of a perfect run through
Raaka-Tu, told as a story. Each paragraph corresponds to a range of
commands in the numbered command list further below, so you can follow
along move-by-move if you want to see exactly what triggers what.

You come to with no memory of how you got here, standing in a dense,
dark, damp jungle. Two cautious steps — north, then west — turn up
nothing but more of the same suffocating undergrowth. (1-2)

A third step west finally breaks the treeline: through the foliage you
glimpse the east wall of a great temple, and a patrol of guards
vanishes around a corner before they spot you. Circling north reveals
the temple's north wall, magnificent and imposing, and pressing west
again brings you within sight of the great bronze gates set into its
western face — engraved with coiling serpents, and, again, guarded.
(3-5)

You slip east through the gates' approach and find yourself standing
before the west entrance itself. At your feet, glinting in the
undergrowth, lies a single coin — you snatch it up before anyone
notices. (6-7)

Retreating back among the trees, you watch and wait. The temple guard
marches its patrol in a slow, repeating rhythm — appearing around one
corner, vanishing around another — and you hold perfectly still,
watching the pattern cycle again and again, counting the guards' steps
until you're sure of your moment. (8-21)

The moment comes. You move north along the treeline to the temple's
north wall and find a great tangle of vines climbing the stone. You
seize them and climb — but the "roof" you scramble onto is nothing of
the sort, and it gives way beneath you. You crash down into a small
granite room, bruised but alive, with a passage east and a jagged hole
in the ceiling above marking your fall. (22-24)

East leads into a grand rectangular hall floored with an oriental rug
and dominated by a huge carved wooden door in the east wall; a hole to
the south drops into a dark, sloping passageway. Following it up and
south, you emerge at the top of a crossroads — corridors running east
and west, and the smell of food nearby. West brings you to a small,
bare room holding nothing but a large sword, which you take without
hesitation. Backtracking east and pressing on, you pass through a
T-shaped junction and into a strange triangular room: a stone statue
brandishing a bow and arrow stands watching an east door, and beneath
a slot cut into the north wall you find an old inscription. On
instinct, you feed your coin into the slot — gears groan, and the
statue slowly pivots to face the west door instead. A path has been
unlocked. (25-33)

East through the newly favored door brings you to the south end of a
long, grand hallway. A precious gem lies in plain sight, and you
pocket it before heading north. At the hallway's north end stands a
heavy door beneath a stone archway — sealed shut, and your first push
against it goes nowhere. But working the door directly does the trick:
it shrieks open on rusted hinges. (34-38)

Beyond it lies a vault. A bejeweled lever juts from one wall beneath a
mounted plaque — and the moment you step inside, the door slams shut
behind you, sealing you in. You wrench the lever free anyway, and with
it in hand you force the door again; this time it grinds open to let
you out, only to swing shut once more the instant you're back in the
hallway. (39-42)

You press on west, into a long dark tunnel lit by nothing but a single
candle and marked overhead with cryptic runes. Deeper still, and
deeper again, the tunnel finally opens onto a large chamber: a
blood-stained altar dominates the room, a golden idol gleams at its
center — and coiled on the floor is a massive serpent, which surges
up and constricts around you before you can react. (43-46)

A desperate, drawn-out fight follows. Your sword sinks to the hilt in
the serpent's scaly flank, but its fangs find you and poison burns
through your veins; you strike again with the same result, poisoned a
second time. Twice more your blade only throws sparks off the stone
wall as the serpent lunges and you barely dodge clear. Finally your
sword connects twice in a row, the second blow proving fatal — the
serpent goes still. Bloodied but victorious, you claim the golden idol
from the altar. (47-53)

You retrace your steps back east through both stretches of tunnel,
through the hallway's north end (door shut fast once more) and south
end, through the T-junction, and south into a room of grey stone with
passages leading north and east. East of that lies a round, high-walled
chamber holding a ring of finest gold. You take it, then — for reasons
only you know — set it back down, and immediately think better of it
and pick it up again. Backtracking west, north through the T-junction,
and east, you arrive in a petite chamber with a larger room to the
north and a passage west. You linger here a long while, watching and
waiting as you did outside the temple gates, until the moment feels
right to move on. (54-78)

North, and the smell of decay hits you before you see anything: a
charnel room, and perched on a ledge above the north passage, a
hideous stone gargoyle. The instant you try to press further north, it
lurches to life and drops to block your path, snapping at your face —
you pull back just in time, and it sinks its teeth into your shoulder
instead. You answer with your sword, and by pure luck the blow finds
its heart; the gargoyle shudders, takes one last breath, and goes
still. Past its corpse, north again, you find a tall stone chamber
holding a golden chopstick, which you add to your haul. (79-83)

From here you retrace the long path home: south past the gargoyle's
carcass, through the petite chamber, the T-junction, the hallway's
south and north ends (the door stubbornly shut again), and back west
through both tunnel stretches to the altar room — where the serpent
now lies still and dead, exactly as you left it. You take a long look
around, then throw your weight against the altar itself. With great
effort it grinds aside, revealing a secret passage beneath. You climb
down into it — and the altar slides back into place above you,
sealing the way you came. (84-95)

The secret passage leads east to a dead end — but there's a hole in
the ceiling here too. You climb up through it, and just like the altar,
the opening seals itself magically behind you the instant you're
through. You emerge at the temple's south wall, out in a quiet clearing,
with the great temple looming one last time behind you. A few final
steps south carry you back into the dense, dark, damp jungle where you
began — treasure in hand, alive, and free. (96-99)

Checking your score confirms it: out of a possible fifty points, you've
earned all fifty. A perfect escape from Raaka-Tu. (100)
